Study In Japan

Admired the hospitality culture of Japanese, loved the serendipity of the picturesque location of Japan, enjoyed the manga culture and had a fascination about tech development of the country. Studying in Japan could be a lifetime opportunity to live those experiences in Japan while growing your career.

Top reasons for studying in Japan

High educational standards:

Japan has the highest number of Nobel Prize winners of any Asian country, and the second highest of any country since 2000.49% of Japanese High School graduates enter university. Japan has over 700 universities, with 10 ranked in the top 200 worldwide. With high quality of education no doubt there is over 150,000 international students study in Japan.

Innovation and Cutting edge technology:

Japan boasts the third-largest economy in the world know for making things smaller, faster and first. The world is amazed how Japan is developing our imagination and making it come true. Huge contribution is made by the research and development oriented education system of Japan.

Learn Japanese while studying:

Though some universities are offering programs in English language but having a proficiency in Japanese language comes as beneficial for living in Japan. It is helpful to develop the bilingual ability in you and it will look appealing in your CV as well.

Low tuition fees and generous scholarships:

With tuition fees being highly affordable a wide range of scholarships are available, both from the university, and from public and private organizations for international students. The government is keen to attract more international students, and has set a target of having foreign students in the country thus has been making procedures for them more affordable and accessible.

Rich and fascinating culture:

Wherever your ventures take you in Japan, you can do so with the peace of mind that comes from knowing you’re in one of the world’s safest countries. Discover peaceful temples and shrines, demon museums, and the history of how Japan was unified by the sword. Enjoy summer festivals and year-round traditional ceremonies, in friendly rural villages and dynamic global cities.

Popular cities in Japan for international students

Tokyo

Japan’s capital Tokyo ranked among the world’s most expensive cities it does not have to be ridiculously bank-breaking. There are more tranquil pleasures to be enjoyed apart from the neon light city experience.

Kyoto

Known as Japan’s cultural capital Kyoto has historical site surrounded by thriving industry and business, as well as a year-round program of festivals and cultural events.

Osaka

Osaka remains a key economic and cultural hub, with a large and diverse population and an economy bigger than some entire countries.
